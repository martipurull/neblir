Introduction to Neblir


The Beam, The Wall and The Old War

A beam of purple, blue and pink shoots into the dark sky. Its diameter is larger than that of Greater London. Its height is impossible to determine, higher than the planet’s atmosphere. The Beam is the Source, the origin and meaning of life as far as Neblirians know. Many fought and died for the land it commands. It now rests beyond the Wall, a brutal barrier thousands of kilometres long from east to west and hundreds of kilometres high. The Supremacy won the Old War and erected the Wall, but that was millenia ago. Nobody knows much else about the Supremacy. Indeed, the sea is protected by a floating Wall that shoots down any vessel that may approach from northern waters.

On this side of the Wall, civilisation recovered and thrived. Settlements became cities, cities became countries, and countries fused into districts of one gargantuan urban sprawl the size of the continent. Only the immediate area north of the Wall and the hardly inhabitable expanse of snow and ice at the very north of the continent aren’t part of the Confederation.

The Buffer

The Buffer is the space between the Wall and the first districts of the Confederation. It is a bright and hot swath of land left to the constant bombing of the Supremacy – a reminder not to get too close. Then, one day, the bombing stopped. Over the past two hundred years, people have been moving there – the poor, the destitute, the persecuted, the dreamers… an amalgamation of undesirables that have grown into their own society, a wealth of ancient runes beneath their feet.

The Outer Confederation

To the north of the north, the Outer Confederation is a sparsely inhabited space, originally home to a handful of scientific outposts. Over the past century, these outposts grew into a semblance of small towns. Some go there exiled from their districts, especially the brutal police state known as the Northern Federation.

The Confederation: Districts and Languages

The Confederation (legally, the Second Confederation, as the First Confederation collapsed in the One Hundred Years War two centuries ago) extends to every other corner of the continent. Its districts have their own histories, cultures and languages, although Linfra (or Common) is spoken or at least understood mostly everywhere. The two oldest districts, Pendence and Deresyst hark back to the years immediately after the Old War ended. They founded the First Confederation and, some would argue, doomed it to fail when they refused to relinquish their firm grip on its direction. They are still rich, affluent districts and enjoy the best weather, but they have lost their edge in favour of more modern and innovative districts such as Shanside, Nasra or the feared Northern Federation.

Good Ol’ Dryth

Dryth (pronounced DRY-th), where our story begins, borders Pendence, one of the original districts. It has always had a close relationship with Pendence, exacerbated by the rivalry with its eastern, maritime neighbour Shanside, which declared its independence centuries ago to become a formidable sea power. Nobody cares much about Dryth other than Drythians and only when they speak with people from other districts. Not much happens there; it’s a good place to start your career if you didn’t get into a prestigious university, but probably not where you’ll get rich, not soon anyway. It depends on Shanside for fresh sea produce and on Pendence for subsidies and investment. Yet, the weather’s good, the streets are mostly safe, and the Siikeball team is one of the best in Neblir.

The Races

Three races inhabit Neblir: the Kinians, the Fenne and the Humans. There is a fourth race, the unlikely offspring of Humans and Fenne: the Manfenn, who are generally distrusted by most because of their eerie looks and strange personalities, having a reputation for having made some of the most terrible leaders Pre-Wall Neblir ever knew.

The Kinians are generally taller than Humans (2m to 2.5m would be the norm), with copper, dark skin, harder than a Human’s, almost plated. They have a special connection to Siike and are considered to be the Original People of the Beam. They can communicate telepathically at shorter distances, but some manage to extend their ability to hundreds of kilometres with those emotionally closest to them. However, they cannot survive too far from the Beam, which has meant they have been mostly excluded from northward expansion. The oldest, most powerful families in the southern districts tend to be Kinian. They account for about 35% of the population.

The Fenne are generally shorter than Humans (1.2m to 1.5m), with six fingers on each hand, including two opposable thumbs. They are dexterous and cunning and have a history of living in close-knit communities, although this has diluted for centuries now. Some Fenne still believe they are the guardians of order, the bridge between the proud Kinians and the chaotic Humans. The worst discrimination of Manfenn is at the hands of such Fenne. Fenne have made the most celebrated craftsmen in Neblir’s history, and some of its most innovative creators and entrepreneurs. They currently account for about 20% of the population.

Humans are the most numerous (about 40% of the population) as they adapt to most climates. They are also notorious for their ambition and ruthlessness, although it would be unfair to limit such common traits to one race.

This leaves the Manfenn with a meagre 5%. They are strange folk, ferociously independent unlike the Fenne, either not ambitious or scared to be, always distrusted, always under suspicion. They say some parents take their Manfenn children to the Outer Confederation to escape prejudice.

The Setting

The urban sprawl in Neblir is such that it isn’t unusual for districts (especially the original ones and those nearest) to have grown up and down, with entire neighbourhoods created underground and others levels above, with hardly any communication between them. One can open a door and enter a different reality, get lost and struggle to find a way back to a “normal” street.

There are fast maglev trains that speed like bullets through tubes that curve around buildings. Those with money can afford their own capsule vehicles, round droplets enough for one person to fit in and fly from place to place. Some capsules are bigger and can fit families, but these are extraordinarily expensive and rare. The streets are bright with all sorts of artificial light – Kinians prefer darker tones, resembling the Beam while Humans are more inclined to brighter and warmer light.

Some notable landmarks include the Great Garden, in Pendence, an immense botanic garden the size of Germany where all sorts of creatures live and where some pay high amounts of money to go on holiday. What about the floating districts? These are towers that rise hundreds of metres into the air and support city-sized exclusive environments for the wealthiest.

Money

Although some other currencies exist, like the Northern Federation’s Nordcoin or Nasra’s Nas, most people in the Confederation use ConfCoin or Conf for short.

The GRID

GRID stands for Grand Radius of Interconnected Devices. Not everybody knows that. It’s the Grid to you and me. When you’re born they may pierce your ears, but, these days, they’ll surely pierce the inner side of your wrist to insert a chip. The GRID grows with a person and displays as their thoughts become intricate and complex enough. For most people, life without the GRID is unimaginable. And yet some, cut them off to flee into exile…

The GRID appears as an overlay in one’s field of vision, superimposed on the real world. You’ll use it to order food, to study, research or communicate with your friends. You don’t need a device, you only need to think about what you want and it’ll flash before you. Businesses will detect you as you walk near their establishments and send you offers. The GRID can get a bit too much, but you wouldn’t know otherwise.

Some are known to use the GRID to attack someone else’s brain, but this is an abominable practice banned by all war laws. Of course, it still happens. For that, one must modify its chip, a practice that’s highly pricey and illegal.

In places like the Buffer and the Outer Confederation, non-chipped people are not unheard of. Illegal chips are also a feature, allowing their users access to the Confederation.

Religion

Although more than half of the population doesn’t hold supernatural beliefs, religion still plays an important role in Neblir. Most religious folks believe in Tritheology, the belief that three divine beings ruled over the forces of chaos and tamed Siike into the Beam, which could then sustain life as we know it. There are many different churches and denominations that explain this same general idea of three creators from different perspectives.

Monotheists also exist, mostly Human, linking the Beam to the one God, the entity that saved all races from extinction and built the Beam for them to worship and protect.

Fenne are mostly concerned with the natural forces, believing in the sacred nature of Siike as a force emanating from the core of the universe, not from some divine being. Some Fenne believe themselves to be the Chosen People, sent to Neblir to establish a balance between Kinians and Humans.

Other religions and cults exist but aren’t incredibly well-known.

A Brief History of Dryth

Like Qadus, In’hara, Humfinn, Deresyst, Pendence and the southernmost part of Shanside, Dryth enjoys the best climate in Neblir. Only the top north-western region is cold.

Dryth formed only 32 years after the original districts, in 1523, a coming together of city-states that had grown into one another and were too far from Pendence to be under its direct control. It once had access to the ocean, but the War of the Water (1646-1658) saw the newer district Shanside (established in 1541 when its city states decided not to join Dryth) conquer the temperate and thus richer coastal end of Dryth (a series of inchoate enclaves then, devoted to fishing and primitive sea mineral extraction) and assimilate it into its territory.

Dryth is a well-behaved district as far as the original districts are concerned. Their politicians have always played along, not causing too much trouble, helping when necessary but not unless formally requested.

The implosion of the Cold Alliance during the One Century War (1802-1902) confirmed the fears posed by the volatility of the north. These fears first entered Dryth when news arrived of the Northern Troubles (1659) two centuries prior (The Northern Belt lost most of its territory to three new districts and to the then-called The North, today the Northern Federation). Dryth saw its closeness to the original districts as essential to preserve its independence regardless of the growing centralism of the First Confederation.

The population of Dryth is mostly conservative and keen on a quiet life, which has traditionally led its leaders to seek a mutually beneficial relationship with the one-time enemy district of Shanside. Dryth refines and processes much of the raw materials and produce Shanside extracts from the Western Ocean. There is still massive rivalry between the two, but they could not imagine ever entering formal conflict with one another: Shanside cannot process everything it generates, and Dryth needs the raw materials its neighbour has an abundance of. Indeed, Dryth makes much of its revenue from the sale of such products to Pendence and Deresyst. Admittedly, after the end of the One Century War, Nasra and the Northern Federation have also become stable clients too, eager to feed their seemingly implaccable progress. For most Drythans, the Northern Federation is the sort of monster you’re happy you no longer have to fight.

In the south, after the 5-century-long, relentless bombing of the Buffer ceased, Dryth and Shanside agreed not to take any territory from the Buffer, but they have both pumped Buffer West with money and resources, competing for influence and allowing this to be one of the most up-and-coming areas in Neblir. Buffer West has most of the pros of a district (decent infrastructure, decent security) without many of the cons (laws, taxes, etc.)

Dryth Zones

Dryth comprises 24 zones, each with its own character and characteristics. Our story begins in Pampers Bar, situated in what is known as New Zone, an up and coming area of new, modern buildings, spacious avenues and chic establishments. District House hosts the district’s government, Banks is home to most financial institutions, Devnoo is brimming with the most advanced tech companies, and then there are the industrial zones of Lyra’th Noo and Ymph, as well as the working class residential areas to the north-west and the high-end neighbourhoods of Central and Souther.
