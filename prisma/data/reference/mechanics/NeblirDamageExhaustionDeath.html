Damage, Exhaustion & Death

Damage

Physical Damage

On a hit, the attacker rolls Damage based on the weapon Damage stat.

A Critical Hit adds Damage dice (see Critical Hits above).

If a hit’s Damage is half or more of the target’s maximum Health Points, the target suffers a Serious Injury (at the GM’s discretion).

If a character sustains three Serious Injuries OR their Health Points are reduced to 1, they must make a Stamina + Resilience save or fall unconscious.

A fourth Serious Injury OR Health Points being reduced to zero makes them fall unconscious automatically. If a fourth Serious Injury also reduces their Health Points to 0, the character dies.

Falling

For falls over 3m, a character sustains 1d6 Physical Damage per 3m. Thus, someone falling from 21m, would take 7d6 Physical Damage.

Agility + Acrobatics checks

A character that falls should make an Agility + Acrobatics check. Every success removes 1d6 from the fall damage.

Moreover, the presence of one success rounds the fall damage down. No successes mean the damage is rounded up. For instance, if a character falls from 20m, and gets 2 successes on an Agility + Acrobatics check, the damage rolled would be 6d6 (20 / 3 rounded down) MINUS 2d6, so 4d6. One success would make it 5d6. No successes would make the damage 7d6, as it is rounded up and no successes can be subtracted from the fall damage.

Psychological Damage

Psychological damage is not necessarily a consequence of combat, although combat often leaves mental scars.

The GM will assess the brutality of a scene or action and determine if the player takes psychological damage.

In addition, players are encouraged to suggest something being traumatic for them to enhance storytelling.

A fourth Serious Trauma or Mental Health Points being reduced to 0 would render a character incapable of making their own decisions – the player would surrender control of them to the GM.

GRID Damage

When dealing GRID Damage, the attacker can choose to inflict Physical or Mental pain on the target. Roll as many d6 as stated by the GRID software used for the attack.

Serious Injuries

A Serious Injury affects how a character behaves physically: their speed may be reduced (halved or depleted and thus cause them to need personal or mechanical assistance to move, etc.), their sight may be impaired or gone altogether, their Attack Rolls may incur penalties (if they lose mobility of an arm, for instance), etc.

Serious Injuries are at the GM’s discretion and must make sense in the context of the game played.

Additionally, sustaining a Serious Injury may offer an opportunity for character development. A player may discuss with the GM and agree to move Attribute points accordingly: for instance, say a player becomes perpetually lame; their speed could be halved, but the player and the GM may agree that moving two points from Agility to Perception and Resilience makes sense as the character has become more observant, cautious and overall stronger at resisting pain.

Serious Trauma

Serious Trauma affects how a character behaves. The trauma should be a discussion between the player and the GM, and it is for the player to role-play how they affect the character’s behaviour.

Additionally, sustaining a Serious Trauma injury may offer an opportunity for character development. A player may discuss with the GM and agree to move Attribute points accordingly: for instance, survivor's guilt may lead a player and GM to agree to move a point from Mentality to Insight as they’ve become less stable but more observant of people’s emotions.

A character may take up to three Serious Trauma injuries. A fourth Serious Trauma injury would render a character incapable of making their own decisions – the player would surrender control of them to the GM.

Damage Penalties

Physical Health Penalties

One serious injury OR 50% of Physical Health Points gone imposes a one-die penalty to any physical rolls.

Two serious injuries OR 75% of Physical Health Points gone impose a two-die penalty on physical rolls and a one-die penalty on psychological rolls.

If a character sustains three serious injuries OR their health points are reduced to 1, they must make a constitution save or fall unconscious.

A fourth serious injury OR health points being reduced to zero makes them fall unconscious.

Mental Health Penalties

One serious trauma or 50% or Mental Health Points gone imposes a one-die penalty to any psychological rolls.

Two serious traumas or 75% of Mental Health Points gone imposes a two-die penalty to any psychological rolls.

A third serious trauma or Mental Health Points reduced to 0 would render a character incapable of making their own decisions – the player would lose control of them and surrender them to the GM.

Exhaustion

Characters need sleep. In Neblir, most people need one phase of sleep (10 Neblirian hours). This usually happens at Third Phase. Adventuring often pushes characters to keep going without rest. However, such strains have consequences:

Points of exhaustion

1 Exhaustion Point: 2d10 penalty on Physical Attributes

2 Exhaustion Points: 2d10 penalty on Physical Attributes AND Mental Attributes.

3 Exhaustion Points: 3d10 penalty on ALL Attributes.

4 Exhaustion Points: 4d10 penalty on ALL Attributes AND Speed reduced by half.

5 Exhaustion Points: 4d10 penalty on ALL Attributes AND Speed reduced by half AND 2d10 penalty on ALL Skills.

6 Exhaustion Points: 4d10 penalty on ALL Attributes AND NO Speed (character is lying down or sitting up AND 2d10 penalty on ALL Skills.

  • If a character doesn’t get proper rest (one phase, typically, at GM’s discretion), the next day imposes one point of exhaustion. Every consecutive day without rest imposes another point. Any Attribute roll will still allow for 1d10 to be rolled.
  • Additionally, external conditions can add to this exhaustion: difficult terrain, sustained climbing or running, etc. An hour of such strenuous activity will count as a Phase (3 Phases make a day). Thus, a character becomes exhausted after a 3-hour run or spending 3 hours in a desert or in frigid conditions.
  • At 6 Exhaustion Points, a character must take 1d6 Physical and 1d6 Mental damage for every Phase in this condition. If they reach 0 Physical HP, they must make Death Saves (see below). If they reach 0 Mental HP, they break down and GM assumes control of the character.

Falling Unconscious

Characters reduced to 0 Physical HP fall unconscious, have no Movement and cannot take Actions or Reactions.

They are also injured and might die if untreated. To determine their fate, they must now make Death Saves (see below).

Drowning

Oxygen

Naturally, a humanoid can hold their breath underwater for 1 minute + 10 seconds per Resilience + Stamina total score. After that time, they must make a Resilience + Stamina roll to add 10 seconds per Success. Repeat the roll at the end of that.

On a failure, take 1d6 physical damage + 1d4 psychological damage.

Repeat the roll every 10 seconds.

Drowning characters automatically fail Death Saves (see below).

Atmospheric Pressure

Without a suit, Humans must make a Resistance (External) + Resilience when under 4 (~30 metres) atmospheres or take 1d6 physical damage for every minute spent at the current atmosphere level. Every new atmosphere demands a new roll and the damage increases by 1d4 per atmosphere.

Death Rolls

If injured and untreated, an unconscious character is dying and must make a Death Roll every turn: roll Resilience OR Stamina MINUS any Serious Injuries they may have sustained (minimum 1 die). Any result of an 8 or above counts as a Success.

After three successful saves, they’re stable for a minute before repeating the process.

After three failed saves, they are dead.

A successful Manual + Medicine check stabilises them for a minute and avoids having to roll Death Saves. The check can be repeated instead of Death Saves every minute. If the check fails, a Death Save must be rolled.

If a character has any Medicine points, a successful check means stabilisation without needing to repeat the Death Saves every minute.

Finding an ambulance or a hospital saves them.

Madness Rolls

If a character is brought down to 0 Mental HP, they must make a Madness Roll every turn: roll Mentality MINUS any Serious Trauma (minimum 1 die). Any result of 8 and above is a Success.

After three successful rolls, they are catatonic for 1 Phase unless treated.

After three failed rolls, they are permanently unresponsive and must be treated at a psychiatric institution.

Professional Intervention

A successful Deduction + Medicine roll gives a character an idea of what to administer to stabilise them: a sedative like CalmX, a stimulant like some illegal Zen, etc. If such a substance is administered before the character fails three rolls, they may regain Mental HP (depending on substance used) and thus be back in control of their faculty.

Friendly Intervention

Alternatively, a Persuasion OR Deception OR Mentality roll has the following effects:

  • No Successes: the catatonic character fails a Madness Roll.
  • 1 Success: nothing happens.
  • 2 Successes: the catatonic character may re-roll a failed Madness Roll.
  • 3 Successes: the catatonic character gains one successful Madness Roll.

Instant Death

If the damage sustained by the character not only reduces them to 0 HP but also doubles their Max Health Score, they die instantly without the possibility of doing Death Saves.

For instance, if a character’s Max Health is 20HP and an explosion deals 45 damage to them, they die instantly even if their Current Health was at its max (20HP). If the character’s Current Health were 10HP and they suffered 25HP of damage, they would be unconscious (they’d be at -15HP, which doesn’t double their Max Health Score, 20HP). However, if they were at 2HP and suffered the same 25HP of damage, they would die instantly, as the damage would take them to -23HP, which is more than double their Max Health Score of 20HP.
